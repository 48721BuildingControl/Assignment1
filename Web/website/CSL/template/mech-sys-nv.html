Natural Ventilation System

In order to grant occupants more access to the nature, the design of CSL involves natural ventilation to provide fresh air with high quality. For all the regularly occupied indoor space, it is designed to have operable windows within 15 feet. It can not only provide occupants with easy access to the nature view, but also allow natural ventilation in those space to reduce energy consumption of HVAC system. The location of operable windows was designed and optimized through a computational fluid dynamics (CFD) study. The thermostat setpoint is designed to be 78°F (25.5°C) instead of conventional 72°F (25.5°C) to maximize the period allowing natural ventilation. For part period of the year, the entire building area can be adequately served by natural ventilation. Atrium space is unconditioned without mechanical heating or cooling while it implements many passive strategies such as thermal massing, phase-change material, high-performance operable glazing, solar shading to maintain temperature within comfortable level. Below is the summary of natural ventilation features:

  • 100% access to operable windows within 15 feet

  • Thermostat setpoint raised to 78°F (25.5°C) for maximizing natural ventilation hour

  • Atrium passive design startegies:thermal massing, phase-change material, high-performance operable glazing, solar shading
