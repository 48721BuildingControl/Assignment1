Daylighting Overview

  • Light from the sun is the primary method of lighting the building during the daytime
  • Building is oriented to maximize daylighting
  • Most spaces in the CSL can be lit solely with daylight 70-80% of the time
  • Floorplan of the CSL designed so more frequently occupied spaces are within 15 feet of windows, increasing their daylight
  • Technologies like light shelves, sloped ceilings, louvers, and overhangs used to project light further into spaces

Light Shelves

  • Used to reflect light farther into the room
  • Light bounces off light shelf and ceiling and ends up further into the room
  • Helps areas farther from windows be lit with daylight

Sloped Ceilings

  • Used in combination with light shelves
  • Slope causes light reflected off ceiling to reflect further into building

Louvers and Overhangs

  • Direct sunlight causes glare and discomfort to occupants
  • Louvers are angled slats that allow light, without direct sunlight, to enter space
  • Overhangs above windows block more light than louvers but also help prevent direct sunlight from entering
