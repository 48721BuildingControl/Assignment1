ABOUT

Located at Phipps Conservatory and Botanical Gardens, the Center for Sustainable Landscapes (CSL) is one of the most sustainable buildings in the world. It was built on a brownfield site and now serves as an education, research, and administration facility for the Phipps Conservatory.

The CSL focuses on the interaction between the built and the natural environment and that of human health and environmental health. Hence, multiple green building technologies concerning energy saving and occupant comfort were implemented, particularly through the mechanical and lighting systems.

The CSL generates all its electricity on-site and treats and manages all storm and sanitary water captured on-site, hence achieving net zero energy and net zero water.

Green Certifications

The CSL is the first and only building to be awarded four of the highest green certifications in the world.

Living Building Challenge

The Living Building Challange is currently the most stringent green building certification in the world. The CSL fulfills requirements for the seven performance areas, "petals", of the Living Building Challenge: place, water, energy, health & happiness, materials, equity, and beauty.

Sustainable SITES Initiative

The CSL is the first and only project to achieve the SITES four star certification for landscapes

LEED Platinum

LEED certification is one the most widely accepted green building rating system in the world. The CSL has achieved the highest level of certification: platinum, and is tied for the highest points awarded to a project under v2.2

WELL Building

The CSL is the first building to be awarded the WELL platinum pilot certification. To achieve this level of certifcation, the project has to meet all of the WELL Preconditions and 80% or more of the Optimization features
