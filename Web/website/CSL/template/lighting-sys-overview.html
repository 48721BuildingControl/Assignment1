Lighting System Overview

The Center for Sustainable Landscapes is lit by a combination of daylighting and electric lights. When there is enough daylight, spaces in CSL are lit without electricity. The lights that were installed were chosen to minimize electricity usage when they must be turned on, though efficient lighting methods (fluorescents), dimming ballasts, and occupancy sensors to turn them off when they are unnecessary. On the Passive Design - Daylighting page you can find more information about the different daylighting techniques used to maximize the amount of daylight penetrating the building. On the Electrical Lighting page you can find more information on the building control system and the lighting system. On the System Location page, you can find more information about individual light fixtures used and their position within the building.

General System Information

  • Level 1 Electric Lighting Installation: January 5, 2012 to January 18, 2012
  • Level 2 Electric Lighting Installation: December 12, 2011 to December 23, 2011
  • Electrical Contractor (for installation): Hanlon Electric
  • MEP/FP Design: CJL Engineering

Daylighting

Using daylight when possible decreases electricity use.

Electrical Lighting

To ensure comfort of the occupants, electrical lighting is also available for when daylight is insufficient.

Lighting Physical Locations

Each type of light and its physical location on all three floors of the building is available under System Diagrams.

Net Zero Energy

Due to the daylighting and electrical lighting systems' efficiency and other energy saving and generating means, the CSL building is net zero energy. More information on this can be found under Sustainability.
