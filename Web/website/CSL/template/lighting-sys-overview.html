Lighting System Overview

The Center for Sustainable Landscapes is lit by a combination of daylighting and electric lights. When there is enough daylight, spaces in CSL are lit without electricity. The lights that were installed were chosen to minimize electricity usage when they must be turned on, though efficient lighting methods (fluorescents), dimming ballasts, and occupancy sensors to turn them off when they are unnecessary. On the Passive Design - Daylighting page you can find more information about the different daylighting techniques used to maximize the amount of daylight penetrating the building. On the Artificial Lighting page you can find more information on the building control system and the lighting system. On the Lighting System page under System Diagrams, you can find more information about individual light fixtures used and their position within the building.

Daylighting

Using daylight when possible decreases electricity use.

Artificial Lighting

To ensure comfort of the occupants, artificial lighting is also available for when daylight is insufficient.

Lighting Physical Locations

Each type of light and its physical location on all three floors of the building is available under System Diagrams.

Net Zero Energy

Due to the daylighting and artificial lighting systems' efficiency and other energy saving and generating means, the CSL building is net zero energy. More information on this can be found under Sustainability.
