Exhaust Duct

Exhaust duct convey potentially harmful contaminants, and hence is:

  • Negatively pressuized
  • Sealed in accordanced with SMACNA Seal Class A (described in HVAC Duct Construction Standards, Metal and Flexible, 2nd Edition)

    • Source: Josh Wentz architectural engineering thesis

    The duct is connected to the AHU to cool the outside air. More info

    • Exhaust grille: Titus 4FL More info
    • Ceiling diffuser: Titus TMSA-AA More info

    Source: Building Controls and Diagnostics S16 Group 3

    Ceiling Return

    The duct collects air from the Under-Floor Air Distribution system and is mixed with the outside air in the AHU.
    More info

    • Return grille: Titus 4FL More info

    Source: Building Controls and Diagnostics S16 Group 3

    Swirl Diffuser

    Diffusers are installed on the floor in the middle of the room to provide more ventilation for occupants.

    • Underfloor diffuser: Titus TAF-R More info

    Source: Building Controls and Diagnostics S16 Group 3

    Linear Diffuser

    The linear diffusers are installed around the perimeter and is composed of two diffusers coupled together, one for supply, one for return.

    • Supply diffuser: Titus TAF-L-V More info
    • Return diffuser: Titus CT-TAF-L More info

    Source: Building Controls and Diagnostics S16 Group 3

    Underfloor Supply

    This duct is underneath the floor level and supplies air to the diffusers to provide for ventilation.
    More info

    Source: Building Controls and Diagnostics S16 Group 3

    Ceiling Supply

    The ceiling supply supplements the Under-Floor Air Distribution system in providing fresh air for ventilation.

    • VAV diffuser: Titus T3SQ-1 More info

    Source: Building Controls and Diagnostics S16 Group 3
