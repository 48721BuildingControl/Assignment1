Geothermal System

General Introduction

A geothermal system is installed as one of the energy conservation approaches and a significant contributor towards the net zero energy consumption achieved by CSL. It primarily works with the rooftop AHU to provide energy for heating and cooling. There are 14 wells installed 510 feet deep and 20 feet on center from well to well. These wells provide warm water during winter and cool water during summer to the AHU to save energy for heating and cooling. It is estimated that these wells are able to capture 70% of the energy use for heating and cooling.

Source by course instructor
