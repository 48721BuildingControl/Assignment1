CSL Mechanical System Overview

Mechanical system combines passive and active design, served as one of the most technically advanced systems in CSL. The heating and cooling of the majority of the building is provided by one variable air volume (VAV) air handling unit (AHU) with maximum supply of 12,400 CFM. The energy load of AHU is further reduced by installing a geothermal ground source heat pump and enthalpy wheel. From the closed loop geothermal system, preconditioned refrigerant is supplied to the AHU through the mechanical system on the first floor. Other unique design features of mechanical system include a raised floor system for distribution of supply air, thermal radiant floor heating system and green roofs implemented to increase thermal mass for stable heating and cooling loads. The heating and cooling for unconditioned space such as atrium is achieved entirely through passive design.

NATURAL VENTILATION

Natural ventilation for unconditioned space (atrium, etc.), achieving passive heating with solar collection and 100% passive cooling.

GEOTHERMAL SYSTEM

Geothermal system used to work with AHU to reduce the heating and cooling load, achiving an estimated 70% heating and cooling energy capture.

UNDERFLOOR DISTRIBUTED SYSTEM

Underfloor Distribution System (UDFS) is implemented to serve the building due to its nature of low-energy consumption and efficiency.

ENERGY-RECOVERY SYSTEM

Energy-Recovery System is served by a rooftop AHU with tri-coil design which serves the UDFS for the entire building.
