Tri-Coil Energy-Recovery System

General Introduction

Tri-coil energy-recovery system, considered as the backbone of the HVAC system, is served by a rooftop air handling unit (AHU), one dedicated rooftop unit serving the underfloor air distribution system for the entire building. Installed by SSM Industries, the unit provides 12,000 CFM supply air and the system includes enthalpy wheel dehumidification and mechanical dehumidification through a recuperative loop that pre-cools and reheats outside air. When ambient temperatures are cooler and drier than indoor temperatures, the economizer cycle of the unit is able to provide free cooling using outdoor air without mechanical refrigeration.

Source by course instructor


System Description

  • Enthalpy wheel removes moistures from incoming hot muggy outside air and lightens the latent load on the cooling coils using cool dry building exhaust air

  • Pre-cooling coil, first of the exhaust air, picks up some of the sensible heat in the warm outside air, further reducing the outside air cooling load

  • Refrigerant cooling coil produces cold and very dry conditioned supply air from the mixed air of outside air and return air from the space

  • The heat captured from the air cooling coil refrigerant is then rejected to the geothermal wells, which is ultimately stored in the ground to use for heating in the following winter

  • The heat picked up by the pre-cooling coil is then transferred to a reheat coil located just downstream of the main cooling coil to warm the supply air a bit for comfort without using any energy

Source: http://ba.conservatory.org/

System Specification

The unit components include:

  • A filter module (MERV 9 pre‐filters and MERV 13 final filters)

  • Recirculation/mixing box module

  • Exhaust fan with VFD

  • Enthalpy wheel module

  • Tri-coil module

  • Supply fan with VFD

  • Water-cooled compressors
