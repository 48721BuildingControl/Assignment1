Tri-Coil Energy-Recovery System

General Introduction

Tri-coil energy-recovery system, considered as the backbone of the HVAC system, is served by a rooftop air handling unit (AHU), one dedicated rooftop unit serving the underfloor air distribution system for the entire building. Installed by SSM Industries, the unit provides 12,000 CFM supply air and the system includes enthalpy wheel dehumidification and mechanical dehumidification through a recuperative loop that pre-cools and reheats outside air. When ambient temperatures are cooler and drier than indoor temperatures, the economizer cycle of the unit is able to provide free cooling using outdoor air without mechanical refrigeration.

System Description

The enthalpy wheel of the Berner Tri-Coil unit is able to remove moistures from incoming hot muggy outside air and to lighten the latent load on the cooling coils using cool dry building exhaust air. Then some of the sensible heat in the warm outside air is picked up in the first of the three coils, a pre-cooling coil, which further reduces the outside air cooling load. Next, the outside air is mixed with return air from the space, which then goes through the refrigerant cooling coil to produce cold and very dry conditioned supply air. The heat captured from the air by the cooling coil refrigerant is then rejected to the geothermal wells, which is ultimately stored in the ground to use for heating in the following winter. In a typical HVAC system, if you raise the supply air temperature, you let in too much moisture, which can cause air quality and comfort issues. The neat trick in the Berner Tricoil unit is that the heat picked up by the pre-cooling coil is then transferred to a reheat coil located just downstream of the main cooling coil. So, the cold dry supply air is warmed up a bit for comfort, but stays nice and dry, and it doesn‚Äôt use any energy to reheat in the process. (Traugott, 2012)
