Under-floor Air Distribution System

General Introduction

CSL primarily implements an Under-Floor Air Distribution (UFAD) system for open-plan office, conference room and other occupied space combined with a typical VAV system for other spaces. The selection of UFAD system is due to the fact that it is efficient and consumes low energy compared to other comparable systems. These features are achieved by supplying air from the bottom, introducing ventilation air directly into breathing zone and allowing heat from internal loads to stratify above the occupants. In addition, since the system is lying underfloor, it does not have much aesthetic impact and meanwhile saves plenty of ceiling space.


System Components

For a typical UFAD system, there are mainly three components:

  • Underfloor Supply Plenum

  • Floor Diffuser

  • Ceiling Return Plenum
